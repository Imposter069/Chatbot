Ayushman Bharat - Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY):

Description: Launched in 2018, PM-JAY is the world's largest health assurance scheme, offering a health cover of ₹5 lakhs per family per year for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization. It targets over 10 crore poor and vulnerable families, approximately 50 crore beneficiaries.

Pradhan Mantri National Dialysis Programme:

Description: Initiated in 2013, NHM encompasses the National Rural Health Mission and the National Urban Health Mission. It aims to provide universal access to equitable, affordable, and quality healthcare services across India.

National Programme for Palliative Care (NPPC):

Description: Established to improve access to palliative care services across India, NPPC focuses on enhancing the quality of life for patients with life-limiting illnesses through pain management and supportive care.

Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY) for Senior Citizens:

Description: In September 2024, the Indian government expanded the PM-JAY to provide free medical insurance to citizens aged 70 and above. This initiative offers an annual coverage of ₹5 lakhs per family, benefiting an additional 60 million senior citizens.

National Leprosy Eradication Programme (NLEP):

Description: Aimed at eliminating leprosy in India by 2027, NLEP focuses on early detection, complete treatment of detected cases, and the prevention of disabilities. The program also emphasizes reducing stigma associated with the disease.

National Leprosy Eradication Programme (NLEP):

Revised National Tuberculosis Control Program (RNTCP)

Description: Now known as the National Tuberculosis Elimination Program (NTEP), this initiative aims to eliminate TB in India by 2025 through early diagnosis, treatment, and public awareness campaigns.
